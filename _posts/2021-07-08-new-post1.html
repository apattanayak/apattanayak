The present document details the notes that I have prepared as I proceed to analyze the NSS Education Survey data.

Let’s import the dataset and order some variables first.

. use "D:\Data\NSS\Education\stata\Merged_data\block1_7_lbl.dta", clear

. order hhid Sl_No_informant Hhsize, before(Person_SN)

We have total of 152,992 individuals who are currently attending. Let’s create an indicator variable current_attend to examine how many of the eligible population are attending currently and those who are not currently attending.

. //indicator: currently attending or not
. recode Enrollment_status_3_35 (4 = 1) (1/3 = 0), gen(current_attend)
(286456 differences between Enrollment_status_3_35 and current_attend)

. label define current_attend 1 "yes" 0 "no"

. label values current_attend current_attend

Let’s also create a variable ever_enroll to indicate how many of the elibigle population ever enrolled.

. //indicator: ever_enrolled
. recode Enrollment_status_3_35 (2/4 = 1) (1 = 0), gen(ever_enrolled)
(286456 differences between Enrollment_status_3_35 and ever_enrolled)

. label define ever_enrolled 1 "yes" 0 "no"

. label values ever_enrolled ever_enrolled

From the above table we see that nearly 78 (=119473/152990) percentage of the currently enrolled population is an unmarried child of the household head. Similarly, we see that nearly 39 (=51428/133463) percentage of the currently not enrolled population is an unmarried child of the household head.

If we consider only the currently enrolled population, then ∼ 95 percent of the population are either unmarried child or grandchild of the household head. Thus for the currently enrolled population alone, we can try to recover the parental education and related information.
